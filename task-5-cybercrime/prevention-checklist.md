

Stay Safe Online: A Prevention Guide for Students
1.Never Share Your UPI PIN to Receive Money: A UPI PIN is strictly required only for
sending or debiting money from your account; you never need to enter your PIN to receive a
payment.
2.Verify "Request Money" Notifications: If you receive a pop-up on a payment app asking
you to "Authorize" or "Pay" to get a refund or payment, decline it immediately—it is a
scam.
3.Check for HTTPS and the Padlock: Before entering any login or personal details on a
website, ensure the URL begins with https:// and shows a locked padlock icon in the
browser bar.
4.Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra layer of security to your
Instagram, LinkedIn, and Gmail accounts so that a password alone is not enough for an
attacker to gain access.
5.Use Unique Passwords for Every Platform: Avoid using the same password for your
college portal and your social media to prevent a "domino effect" if one account is breached.
6.Avoid Financial Transactions on Public Wi-Fi: Never use open Wi-Fi networks in cafes or
stations to access your bank account or UPI apps, as these networks can be easily monitored.
7.Think Before You Click: Be wary of "too good to be true" internship offers or lottery wins
sent via WhatsApp or SMS, as these are often phishing attempts to steal your data.
8.Keep Your Devices Updated: Regularly update your smartphone's operating system and
security apps to protect against the latest malware and ransomware threats.
